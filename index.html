Multi-GradSpeech

Multi-GradSpeech: Towards Diffusion-based Multi-Speaker Text-to-speech Using Consistent Diffusion Models

Heyang Xue, Shuai Guo, Pengcheng Zhu, Mengxiao Bi
Fuxi AI Lab, NetEase Inc., Hangzhou, China

arxiv

ABSTRACT

Recent advancements in diffusion-based acoustic models have revolutionized data-sufficient single-speaker Text-to-Speech~(TTS) approaches, with Grad-TTS being a prime example. However, these methods struggle with multi-speaker TTS tasks, as data-sufficient speakers experience performance degradation in modeling, while data-insufficient speakers suffer from over-smoothing of generated results. Fine-tuning is a common technique to mitigate these issues. To specifically address multi-speaker TTS scenarios, we present Multi-GradSpeech, a diffusion-based acoustic model. Our approach introduces the Consistent Diffusion Model (CDM) as a generative modeling method for diffusion-based acoustic models. We enforce the consistency property of CDM during the inference process, resulting in significant improvements in multi-speaker TTS performance. Our experimental results corroborate that our proposed method can maintain or improve the performance of different speakers involved in multi-speaker TTS, even outperforming the fine-tuning approach.
